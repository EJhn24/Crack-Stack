Group Activity 1 Webinar

I. Event Overview
The webinar titled "Software Engineering and System Architecture" was held on September 30, 2024, from 2:00 PM to 3:30 PM via Google Meet and Facebook Live. Delivered by Mr. Jay Renz Morgado, the session welcomed students from various year levels, focusing on those in the College of Computer Studies. The discussion introduced fundamental concepts of software engineering and system architecture, highlighting best practices and industry relevance.

II. Learnings and Reflections
The speaker broke down complex topics such as the software development lifecycle, design patterns, and the importance of scalability and maintainability. His real-world examples made the technical ideas relatable. The webinar also covered modern methodologies like Agile and DevOps, fostering a better understanding of collaboration in development environments.

III. Practical Applications
Concepts like modular design, architecture diagrams, and structured documentation can be directly applied to academic or personal software projects. As an organizer, these insights are also useful for planning future events, using a more systematic approach like software project management to ensure clarity and success.

IV. Positive Feedback on Organization
The event had a strong turnout of 120 participants and was praised for its smooth execution, informative content, and effective promotion. Attendees appreciated the clarity of the speaker and the Q&A session, which allowed for deeper interaction and learning.

V. Suggestions for Improvement
To further improve future webinars, we suggest incorporating interactive elements such as polls or discussions, extending the Q&A session, and having technical backup plans to avoid disruptions in virtual setups.

Group Activity 2 Seminar

I. Event Overview
The seminar titled "Unlocking the Power of Branding: Design, Identity, and Impact" will be held on October 26, 2024, from 3:00 PM to 4:30 PM in Room 405 at Gordon College Campus, Olongapo City. Led by Mr. Rosmach Bautista, the event is open to students from the College of Computer Studies at Gordon College, encompassing all year levels and courses, as well as external students from other schools. The seminar aims to provide valuable knowledge on brand design, encouraging creativity and highlighting its impact on the industry. Additionally, it will give students a deeper understanding of what to expect in the field of Multimedia. This is an excellent opportunity for aspiring professionals to explore the potential of branding in shaping their future careers.

II. Learnings and Reflections
The seminar highlighted the importance of branding in creating a strong identity and fostering a connection with audiences. Key takeaways included understanding the principles of effective design, the psychological impact of colors and shapes, and the significance of consistency in brand identity. Mr. Bautista emphasized the need for creativity and innovation to stand out in a competitive industry, particularly in the IT and multimedia sectors. I also learned how branding is not just about aesthetics but about storytelling and creating a lasting impression.

III. Practical Applications
The insights gained from the seminar can be applied in practical scenarios such as creating personal branding for job applications or freelance projects. By integrating design principles shared during the talk, I feel more equipped to develop logos, visual materials, and branding strategies that align with target audience expectations. Additionally, I plan to apply these concepts to academic projects, such as designing presentations or collaborative multimedia outputs, to ensure they are professional and impactful.

IV. Positive Feedback on Organization
The event was well-organized, with clear communication and a conducive environment for learning. Mr. Bautista’s expertise and engaging delivery made the session informative and inspiring. The seminar successfully balanced theoretical knowledge with practical insights, keeping the audience involved through relatable examples and interactive discussions.

V. Suggestions for Improvement
While the seminar was effective, incorporating hands-on activities or live demonstrations of branding design would enhance engagement and solidify understanding. Extending the duration of the seminar to allow for a Q&A session would also provide students an opportunity to address specific queries. Lastly, providing digital resources or templates post-event would help attendees implement what they learned more effectively.

Group Activity 3 Seminar

I. Event Overview
The seminar titled "The Importance of Version Control in System Development and Thesis Project" will be held on November 7, 2024, from 2:30 PM to 3:30 PM in Room 520 at Gordon College Campus, Olongapo City. Led by Mr. Ralph Angelo Jaminal, the event is designed for students from various year levels and courses in the College of Computer Studies. The seminar aims to equip participants with essential skills and knowledge to use version control systems effectively, with a particular focus on GitHub. By attending, students will learn how to enhance project management, foster collaboration, and boost productivity in their thesis or development work. This session is a valuable opportunity to master tools critical for success in system development and academic projects.

II. Learnings and Reflections
The seminar highlighted the significance of version control systems, emphasizing how tools like GitHub can streamline collaboration and manage complex projects effectively. I learned that version control is not just a technical requirement but a foundational skill in system development and thesis projects. Concepts such as branching, committing changes, resolving merge conflicts, and maintaining a clear repository history were explained with practical examples. Mr. Jaminal’s insights helped demystify GitHub's intimidating interface, making it accessible even for beginners. Additionally, I gained a better understanding of how version control ensures accountability and traceability in projects, allowing developers to backtrack and identify specific changes. The importance of maintaining organized repositories for future scalability and team collaboration was also emphasized, which I found particularly relevant for thesis work that often involves group dynamics.

III. Practical Applications
I plan to incorporate version control into my thesis project immediately. Using GitHub will enable my team to collaborate more efficiently, avoid redundant work, and minimize conflicts during code integration. By setting up a shared repository and assigning clear responsibilities, we can ensure a structured workflow. On a broader level, the knowledge gained from the seminar can also be applied to internships or freelance projects, where version control is often a standard practice. Maintaining a personal GitHub portfolio can serve as a valuable asset in showcasing my skills and completed projects to potential employers.

IV. Positive Feedback on Organization
The seminar was well-organized, with a clear agenda and a speaker who was both knowledgeable and engaging. The use of live demonstrations made the session interactive and easy to follow. The inclusion of practical examples and real-world applications of GitHub was particularly commendable, as it bridged the gap between theory and practice. The allotted time was used effectively, leaving room for a brief Q&A session, which clarified many doubts.

V. Suggestions for Improvement
The seminar was informative, but future sessions could benefit from hands-on activities for managing GitHub repositories and more time to cover advanced topics like CI/CD pipelines. Overall, it was a valuable initiative that equipped students with important skills, helping them become more proficient with industry-standard tools.

Group Activity 4 Webinar

I. Event Overview
The webinar titled "Introduction to Virtual Assistant (VA) Roles in the Digital Age" will take place on November 23, 2024, from 2:00 PM to 3:30 PM, conducted virtually via Google Meet and Facebook Live. Presented by Mrs. Ruslene C. Salvatus, this session is open to students from various schools and year levels, particularly those pursuing courses related to the College of Computer Studies. The seminar will provide an overview of virtual assistant roles, highlighting their growing importance in different sectors. It will showcase how businesses and individuals can leverage VAs to optimize operations, discuss the essential skills and tools for successful VA work, and explore how VA roles may evolve with advancing technology. This is an excellent opportunity for students to gain insights into a rapidly expanding career field.

II. Learnings and Reflections
As an organizer, one of the most striking takeaways from the webinar was the emphasis on versatility and skill development for VAs. Mrs. Salvatus highlighted the demand for time management, communication, and proficiency in productivity tools like project management software and cloud-based platforms. I also learned about the vast opportunities available to VAs, ranging from administrative support to social media management, which can cater to diverse industries and individual needs. The session underscored the role of technology in reshaping work dynamics, demonstrating how VAs can provide innovative solutions to businesses of all sizes. This broadened my understanding of how virtual roles are not only viable but thriving in today's economy.

III. Practical Applications
The insights from this webinar can be practically applied in multiple ways. For instance, students can start honing their digital skills through accessible tools like Google Workspace, Canva, and Trello to build their VA competencies. As a student, I now see the importance of cultivating a strong online presence and portfolio to showcase my skills to potential employers or clients. Additionally, the strategies for effective time management and multitasking discussed in the webinar are directly applicable to academic life and future work environments. On a broader scale, the event inspired me to consider virtual assistant services as a part-time opportunity while studying, as it offers both flexibility and valuable experience in the professional world.

IV. Positive Feedback on Organization
The webinar was well-organized, with seamless coordination across platforms. The choice of speaker, Mrs. Salvatus, was commendable, as her expertise and engaging presentation kept the audience captivated. The use of Google Meet for real-time interaction and Facebook Live for extended reach ensured accessibility for participants. The event’s structure, beginning with an introduction to VA roles and moving into specific skills and tools, was logical and informative. The Q&A session allowed attendees to clarify doubts and interact with the speaker, making the session dynamic and participatory.

V. Suggestions for Improvement
While the event was successful, there are a few enhancements that could further improve future webinars. Introducing pre-event engagement activities, such as sharing preparatory materials, teaser content, short videos, or quizzes, could build anticipation and better prepare attendees for the session. Adding interactive elements, like live polls or hands-on exercises, during the webinar would boost engagement and ensure participants remain actively involved. Additionally, offering follow-up activities, such as access to session recordings or supplementary resources, would reinforce the learning experience and encourage practical application of the insights gained.

Group Activity 5 Seminar

I. Event Overview
The seminar titled “GCCC Tech Fest 2025” was held on February 22, 2025, at Marikit Park, Olongapo City. It served as a platform for students and tech enthusiasts to explore topics in cybersecurity and artificial intelligence. The event featured insightful talks from experts such as Mr. Domingo J. Chiro Jr. (Cybersecurity 101), Mr. Wilson W. Censon (How AI is Shaping Our Future: Opportunities for Students), and Mr. Marvin Calingacion (Pillars of Information Security). Participants, including students from various institutions and IT professionals, engaged in discussions, competitions, and e-sports tournaments. The Tech Fest aimed to inspire, educate, and connect individuals by showcasing innovations, encouraging collaboration, and creating networking opportunities with industry professionals.

II. Learnings and Reflections
The seminar provided a comprehensive understanding of modern cybersecurity and AI developments. Mr. Chiro’s Cybersecurity 101 emphasized protecting data with strong passwords, encryption, and secure access. Mr. Censon’s AI session revealed the transformative role of AI in sectors like education, business, and healthcare, inspiring me to explore AI-driven solutions. Mr. Calingacion discussed the core principles of information security—confidentiality, integrity, and availability—highlighting their importance in both personal and organizational settings. Beyond the talks, the competitions and gaming events like Mobile Legends and Tekken tournaments demonstrated the strong connection between entertainment, technology, and community building.

III. Practical Applications
The insights gained from the seminar are applicable in several ways. I plan to adopt essential cybersecurity practices such as regularly updating software, using firewalls, and implementing secure authentication. In future development projects, I aim to incorporate the principles of information security to safeguard sensitive data. Additionally, Mr. Censon’s discussion encouraged me to integrate AI tools into academic and professional tasks—such as automating repetitive processes or building smarter applications—enhancing both efficiency and innovation in my work.

IV. Positive Feedback on Organization
The GCCC Tech Fest 2025 was well-organized and engaging. The balance between educational content and interactive activities created an energetic and inclusive atmosphere. The featured speakers delivered their sessions with clarity and relevance, and the event ran smoothly across all segments. The cosplay contest, tech exhibits, and e-sports tournaments added a fun and competitive edge, attracting a wider audience and promoting camaraderie among participants. The organizers ensured that each component of the event was executed efficiently, making it a memorable experience for all attendees.

V. Suggestions for Improvement
To further enhance future Tech Fest events, incorporating hands-on workshops—such as cybersecurity simulations or AI mini-projects—would offer more immersive learning experiences. Attendees would benefit from practical exposure to securing systems or building AI models. Additionally, creating more structured networking opportunities, like breakout sessions or industry-led roundtable discussions, would help participants connect, collaborate, and deepen their understanding of the topics covered.

One of the most notable talks was “Behind AI: The Role of High-Performance Computing”, delivered by Mr. Jazz Urog. The session explored how HPC infrastructure supports the growth and efficiency of modern AI systems, enabling them to learn and operate at scale.

II. Learnings and Reflections
This webinar gave me a clearer understanding of the foundational role HPC plays in AI development. I learned that:

  • Massive datasets and complex AI models require not only powerful CPUs/GPUs, but also optimized memory and storage systems.
  • HPC allows for real-time data processing and greatly reduces training time for deep learning models.
  • Parallel processing and distributed computing are essential for scaling AI applications efficiently.

Mr. Urog also introduced the ethical and environmental concerns surrounding HPC systems, reminding us of the need to strike a balance between innovation and sustainability.

III. Practical Application of Learnings
This session motivated me to explore how computational efficiency can be implemented in my own projects. While full-scale HPC systems may not be accessible at the student level, I can begin practicing principles of:

  • Optimized algorithm design
  • Parallel computing using tools like CUDA or OpenMP
  • Using GPU-accelerated platforms like TensorFlow with GPU support
  • Exploring open-source tools that mimic HPC environments

It also emphasized the importance of computational literacy and the need to better understand how to design efficient, performance-oriented systems even on smaller scales.

IV. Feedback on the Event
The organization of the webinar was highly commendable. The speaker presented the topic in a clear and engaging manner, supported by well-prepared slides and real-world examples. The session was interactive, thanks to the Q&A portion, and the use of visual aids helped in understanding complex topics. The event was well-timed and the platform was stable and accessible throughout.

  • The importance of correct wiring and reading basic circuit diagrams.
  • The significance of safety and best practices when handling electronic components, which is often overlooked but crucial.
  • How user-friendly Arduino is, which makes simple projects like blinking an LED or using a button input very rewarding for beginners.

III. Practical Application of Learnings

  • Exploring more real-world applications of Arduino to build my skills further.
